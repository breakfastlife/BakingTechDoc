Introduction To Baking

A good introduction to baking should always include a simple what to expect not someones lifestory. For instance, on a recipe it should start with the desciption of the item you're baking not how someone's mother use to make this for them after a long day at the beach after the family went camping in Hawaii or something like that.

For these tutorials, I will give you a list of ingridients, a list of tools needed, baking instructions, and then a section on how to improve, improvise, or substitute ingridients.

Before we go on, please remember that everyone starts somewhere so if you aren't great from the beginning then keep practicing and you'll eventually get the hang of it. Start small and you'll do great.

For the introduction, I'm going to go over some safety.

  • For one, if you need to cut anything make sure you do not cut yourself.
    • If you need to watch a video on proper knife work here is a good one.
  • When using the stove or oven, make sure you do not touch anything hot with your bare hands.
  • Please us an oven mit when handling hot items.
    • If you do not have an oven mitt, you can use a dish rag.
    • Warning: make sure you can grip the item properly while using the rag before the item is hot so you know if this will work or not.

Note: Be sure to read the list of tools and ingredients before starting to make sure you have everything you need. /nl

Baking cookies
Peanut Butter Cookies

In this section we will making some simple, peanut butter cookies. We could go with something more traditional and well known... and we will in the second portion of this section. This first portion is to teach you how to bake a cookie and what better way than with little ingriedient so if you mess up it's a big deal.

Ingredients
  • 1 cup peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • Optional: 1 Tbsp (Tablespoon) of Vanilla Extract
Tools Needed
  • 1 bowl
  • 1 whisk or electric mixer
  • 1 baking sheet
  • Non-stick spray or parchment paper
  • 1 fork
  • Measuring Cups
Baking Instructions Step 1

Preheat the Oven to 350 degrees F (or 175 degrees C)

Preheating the oven is crutial as you want the oven to be up to temp when you start baking. If its too cold the cookies wont bake correctly.

Step 2

Mix the peanut butter, sugar, and egg together in the bowl until smooth.

If you're using the optional vanilla, add it in with the rest of the ingridients.

Step 3

Make the baking sheet non-stick using one of the following meathods:

  • Spray baking sheet with non-stick spray.
  • Cover baking sheet with parchment paper.
  • Spray baking sheet with non-stick spray and then put parchment paper over the spray.

Note: using parchment paper is the easy clean up/less dishes method.

Step 4

Roll some of the batter into 1-inch balls and place them at least 1-inch apart.

Step 5

Flatten each using the fork in a criss-cross pattern

Step 6

Bake until firm, about 10 minutes. Let cool on baking sheet before attempting to remove them. Then cool them off even more.

Step 7

Repeat steps 4-6 until all batter is baked into cookies.

How to Modify the Peanut Butter Cookies

One way to change or modify the peanut butter cookies is to add some complimentary flavors. One of which I put as an optional ingredient, vanilla. I personally tend to add a Tbsp of cinnamon as it adds a depth of flavor that compiments the peanut butter flavor. If you need to substitute eggs, you can do one of the following:

  • You can use 1 tsp (teaspoon) of baking soda and 1 Tbsp of vinegar (Per Egg)
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk

Chocolate Chip Cookies

In this section we try the slightly harder cookie recipe. The chocolate chip cookie is a traditional cookie recipe. The cookies are delicious and have plenty of ways to modify the recipe.

Ingredients
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, leveled
    • Scoop using a measuring cup then use the flat end of a butter knife to scrape the excess flour back into the container.
  • 1 tsp (teaspoon) baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1 cup granulated sugar (white)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon
  • 2 cups Chocolate Chips (14 oz package) ( I tend to use 1 Cup as I do not like too much chocolate)
Tools Needed
  • 2 bowls (1 large, 1 medium)
  • Electric mixer (kitchenaid mixer or hand)
  • baking sheet(s) (depends on if you want to wait for the cookies to cool before putting in your next batch)
  • Parchment paper
  • 1 spatula
  • Measuring Cups
Baking Instructions Step 1

Preheat oven to 375 degees F. Line the baking sheet(s) with parchment paper.

Step 2

In a medium bowl mix flour, baking soda, baking powder and salt. Set aside.

Step 3

Cream together butter and sugars until combined.

  • Creaming means, mix at high speeds (using electric mixer) until fully combine and has the consistancy of cream.
  • In my own expirience, this typically takes about 5 minutes.
Step 4

Beat in eggs and vanilla until light color (about 1 minute).

Step 5

Mix the dry ingriedients into the wet bowl a little at a time until combined

  • Try to mix about 1/4 of the dry ingridients at a time.
Step 6

Add chocolate chips and mix well.

Step 7

Roll 2-3 Tablespoons (depending on how large you like your cookies) of dough at a time into balls and place them evenly spaced on your prepared cookie sheets.

  • Try to give them 1 - 2 Inches of space between each cookie. Chocolate chip cookies tend to spread out as they're baking.
Step 8

Bake in preheated oven for approximately 8-10 minutes. Take them out when they are just barely starting to turn brown.

Step 9

Let them sit on the baking pan for 2 minutes before removing to cooling rack.

Step 10

Repeat steps 7-9 until all the batter is baked.

How to Modify the Peanut Butter Cookies

One way to change or modify the cookies is to replace the chocolate chips with another ingridient. When I make peanut butter cookies, I will actually make this recipe and then replace the chocolate chips with 1 cup of peanutbutter. This will make soft and buttery peanut butter cookies. You can also mix in white chocolate chips, butterscotch chips, dark chocolate, chopped up chocolate bars, caramel, m&m's, vanilla chips, ect. These cookies are a very versitile cookie and you can literally just replcae 1 ingredient and get so many variations. You can also add 1/2 cup - 2/3 cup of cocoa powder into the batter and it'll make it into a chocolate base cookie with chocolate chips.

Vanilla Cake

In this section we will making a simple vanilla cake. This first portion is to teach you how to bake a cake and and do some basic frosting work.

Ingredients Cake Ingridients
  • 2 cups plain / all purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 4 large eggs (50 – 55g / 2 oz each), at room temp
  • 1 1/2 cups caster / superfine sugar (granulated/regular ok too)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter , cut into 1.5cm / 1/2” cubes (or so)
  • 1 cup milk
  • 3 tsp vanilla extract (if you're liking baking, buy some good vanilla (warning it wont be cheap))
    • Side note, you can use vanilla beans for this as well, it will be VERY flavorful.
  • 3 tsp vegetable or canola oil
Vanilla Buttercream Frosting
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter , softened
  • 1 lb soft icing sugar / powdered sugar , SIFTED
  • 3 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 – 4 tbsp milk , to adjust thickeness
Tools Needed
  • Mixing bowls
  • Measuring cups
  • cake pans, 2 8" round cake pans or 1 9x13" cake pan
  • sifter
  • stand mixer
Baking Instructions Step 1

Place the oven shelf in the middle of the oven.

Step 2

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.

Step 3

Grease 2 8" cake pans (or 1 13 x 9" rectanglular cake pan [bake time will be 30 minutes])

Step 4

Whisk flour, baking powder and salt in a large bowl. Set aside.

Step 5

Beat eggs for 30 seconds on speed 6 of a Stand Mixer fitted with a whisk attachment, or hand beater.

Step 6

With the beater still going, pour the sugar in over 45 seconds.

Step 7

Then beat for 7 minutes on speed 8, or until tripled in volume and white.

Step 8

Heat Milk-Butter:

  • While egg is beating, place butter and milk in a heatproof jug and microwave 2 minutes on high to melt butter (or use stove).
  • NOTE: Do not let milk bubble and boil (foam ok). Don’t do this ahead and let the milk cool (this affects rise).

Step 9

Gently add flour:

  • When the egg is whipped, scatter 1/3 flour across surface, then beat on Speed 1 for 5 seconds.
  • Add half remaining flour, then mix on Speed 1 for 5 sec.
  • Add remaining flour, then mix on Speed 1 for 5 – 10 sec until the flour is just mixed in.
  • Once you can’t see flour, stop straight away.

Step 10

Lighten hot milk with some Egg Batter:

  • Pour hot milk, vanilla and oil into the now empty flour bowl.
  • Add about 1 1/2 cups (2 ladles or so) of the Egg Batter into the Milk-Butter (don't need to be 100% accurate with amount).
  • Use a whisk to mix until smooth – you can be vigorous here. Will look foamy.

Step 10

Slowly add milk:

Turn beater back on Speed 1 then pour the Milk mixture into the Egg Batter over 15 seconds, then turn beater off.

Step 11

Scrape and final mix:

  • Scrape down sides and base of bowl.
  • Beat on Speed 1 for 10 seconds – batter should now be smooth and pourable.

Step 12

Pour batter into pan(s)

Step 13

Knock each cake pan on the counter 3 times to knock out big bubbles

Step 14

Bake 30 minutes or until golden and toothpick inserted into center comes out clean.

Step 15

Remove from oven. Cool in cake pans for 15 minutes, then gently turn out onto cooling racks.

  • If using as layer cakes, cool upside down – slight dome will flatten perfectly.
  • Level cake = neat layers.

Making the Buttercream Step 16

Beat butter with paddle attachment in stand mixer for 3 minutes on high until it changes from yellow to almost white, and it becomes fluffy and creamy.

Step 17

Add icing sugar / powdered sugar gradually in 3 lots, beating slowly (to avoid a powder storm) then once mostly incorporated, beat on high for a full 3 minutes until fluffy.

Step 18

Add vanilla and milk, then beat for a further 30 seconds.

  • Use milk only if needed to make it lovely and soft but still holds it's form (eg for piping).
  • Use immediately.
  • If you make ahead, refrigerate then beat to re-fluff.

Step 19

Frost the cake using the butter cream. Try to make it even.

How to Modify the Vanilla Cake

One way to modify the vanilla cake is to not use buttercream frosting. You can top it with fresh jam, cream and fresh fruit, chocolate frosting, ice cream, caramel, a crumble topping, lemon glaze, a glaze with chopped nuts, honey, ect.

Baking brownies

In this section we will making a simple brownie.

Ingredients
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 4 eggs
  • ½ cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup walnut halves
Tools Needed
  • Mixing bowls
  • Measuring cups
  • cake pans, 2 8" round cake pans or 1 9x13" cake pan
  • sifter
  • stand mixer
Baking Instructions Step 1

Preheat oven to 350 degrees F and Grease a 9x13 cake pan.

Step 2

Mix sugar, flour, melted butter, eggs, cocoa powder, vanilla, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl until combined.

Step 3

Spread the batter into the prepared pan.

Step 4

Decorate with walnut halves.

Step 5

Bake in the preheated oven until top is dry and edges have started to pull away from the sides of the pan, about 20 to 30 minutes.

Let cool before slicing into squares.

How to Modify the brownies

Instead of topping the brownies with nuts, you can top it with caramel, chocolate chips, chrushed oreos with melted white chocolate.

Baking bread

In this section we will making a French bread.

Ingredients
  • 2 ¼ cups warm water, 110-115 degrees F
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon instant or active dry yeast
    • instant is easier
    • active dry yeast doesnt need to be soaked but I hear a lot of people soak it first so its up to you
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, canola oil, vegetable oil or avocado oil
  • 5 ½ – 6 cups all-purpose flour or bread flour
Tools Needed
  • Mixing bowls
  • Measuring cups
  • 1 9x13" cake pan
  • Parchment paper
  • Razor Blade or Sharp Knife
  • stand mixer
Baking Instructions Step 1

In the bowl of an electric stand mixer fitted with the dough hook, combine the water sugar and yeast.

  • If using active dry yeast, let the mixture bubble and foam before proceeding (this can take 3-5 minutes).
  • If using instant yeast, proceed with the recipe (no need to let the yeast activate).
Step 2
  • Add the salt, oil and 3 cups of flour and mix.
  • Add in 2 1/2 to 3 more cups of flour gradually.
  • The dough should clear the sides of the bowl and form a soft ball that doesn't leave a lot of dough residue on your fingers.
Step 3
  • Knead for 2-3 minutes until the dough is smooth.
  • If the dough starts to cling to the sides of the bowl (or the center column if using a Bosch or other mixer with a center), add 1/4 cup of flour at a time until a sturdy but soft ball of dough forms.
Step 4

Choose the method for the rising method.

  • Rising Method 1: Leave the dough in the mixer, cover with a lid or towel, and let the dough rest for 10 minutes. Stir it down by turning on the mixer for 10 seconds or so. Repeat the "rest and stir down" cycle five more times.
  • Rising Method 2: Instead of letting the dough rest for 10 minute spurts and then stirring it down, transfer the dough to a lightly greased bowl and cover with a towel or greased plastic wrap. Let the dough rise until doubled, about an hour or so, depending on the warmth of your kitchen.
Step 5
  • Turn the dough onto a lightly greased surface and divide in half.
  • Pat each section into a thick rectangle, 9X13-inches or thereabouts (doesn't have to be exact).
  • Roll the dough up starting from the long edge, pressing out any air bubbles or seams with the heel of your hand, and pinch the edge to seal.
  • Arrange seam side down on a large baking sheet lined with parchment paper (I use separate baking sheets for each loaf).
Step 6

>With a bread lame, razor blade or VERY sharp knife, cut several gashes at an angle on the top each loaf (you can wait to score the bread until after it rises, but it can easily deflate if the razor/knife isn't sharp enough).

Step 7

Cover the loaves with greased plastic wrap or a kitchen towel, and let rise until noticeably puffy and nearly doubled in size, about an hour.

Step 8

Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F and make sure an oven rack is in the center position. If you find your bread isn't browning as much as you like, preheat the oven to 400 or 425 degrees and/or move the oven rack up one position (watch carefully so the bread, especially the bottom, doesn't burn).

Step 9

Optional: for an extra golden, crisp crust, place the loaves in the preheated oven and immediately toss 3-4 ice cubes on the bottom of the oven. Close the oven door quickly but gently.

Step 10

Bake the loaves for 25-30 minutes until golden and baked through.

How to Modify the brownies
  • If desired, brush melted butter over the hot loaves (this softens the crust a bit, so if you want a crispier crust, don't butter the top).
  • If you want to use Whole Wheat Flour, add 2-3 minutes of kneading so the gluten can develop.
